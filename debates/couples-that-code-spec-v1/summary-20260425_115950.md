## Key conclusions (both agents converged)

The spec was **stacked fragility** — six unproven hypotheses (scarcity, intimacy, hospitality, instruction, privacy, AI convenience) sold as one SKU before any single one was validated as the reason to buy. The locked v1.4 plan replaces the original spec wholesale:

1. **No lodging in v1.** Drop "couples retreat" framing. Sell a portable services product into existing partner venues. The lodging premise is downstream of proving demand for the ritual.
2. **Three-SKU Phase 0 demand test, not a single bundle:**
   - `Remote Sprint` — 3hr facilitated video session, weekly
   - `Venue Day` — 8hr at one fixed partner venue (one city, one audited backup), alternate weeks
   - `Weekend Retreat` — refundable deposit only, **not delivered in Phase 0**
3. **Hard operational definitions before launch** — `raw matched inquiry`, `qualified lead`, `warm buyer` (defined as a couple that *completed* a delivered SKU, not just paid).
4. **Pass criteria are per-SKU + dual-denominator** (deposit/qualified-lead and deposit/raw-inquiry), not aggregate. Kill rule: 3 campaign variants × 90 days, no SKU clears the bar → kernel is dead.
5. **Founder couples = first cohort, NOT validation cohort.** A second-cohort guard requires non-founder conversion ≥50% of founder conversion before passing Phase 0.
6. **Permanent drops:** managed-token resale; "your IP stays yours" as a brand pillar (demoted to a feature bullet about the physical space); 5–10 lighthouse properties; any supply plan that outruns facilitator capacity.
7. **Held back until after Phase 1 passes:** brand name (use bland placeholder), press, lodging ownership, lighthouse properties, certification, romance framing in headline copy.
8. **Phase 1 unit-economics gate:** ≥50% contribution margin AND first-order CAC payback in one booking AND (≥30% rebook in 12 months OR ≥0.5 organic referrals/booking). All required. A low-frequency premium service that needs paid reacquisition every cycle is dead even at healthy margin.

## Key disagreements (mostly resolved by Round 5)

- **Original Agent A wanted to "hold the bundle for 12 months"** as brand discipline. Reviewer called this premature refusal and self-handicapping. Resolved: bundle is dropped immediately; multi-SKU from day 1.
- **Original Agent A treated founder-taught as discipline.** Reviewer called it evidence the product may not be real ("if magic only exists when a founder runs it, there is no product, only bespoke service"). Resolved: founders teach, but supply scales with facilitator capacity, not with property count.
- **Original Agent A wanted to keep the "Pair Shipping Evening" as the decisive kill signal.** Reviewer pushed back: evening and weekend are different jobs; one passing or failing tells you little about the other. Resolved: weekend retreat is a *demand probe* (refundable deposit) only; not operated in Phase 0.
- **Architect tried to keep "founder couples" as both first cohort and stop-condition.** Reviewer flagged this as identity-driven niche risk. Resolved: explicit second-cohort guard added.
- **Lasting unresolved:** the romance framing. Both agents agreed to defer it (A/B test in body copy only, no headline) but neither argued whether a "build-together" SKU stripped of romance becomes a different company.

---

## Best ideas (most actionable / novel)

1. **Three-SKU isolation test from the same landing page** — separates "do they pay for momentum?" from "do they pay for place?" from "do they pay for escape?" The original spec conflated all three.
2. **Hard operational definitions for the funnel denominators** — without `raw matched inquiry` defined before launch, conversion rates are storytelling. Reviewer's pre-launch lock on these definitions is the single biggest measurement-hygiene fix.
3. **Warm-buyer-only weekend deposits** — measuring weekend interest off cold traffic produces noise; measuring it off couples who *completed* a delivered SKU is the only honest signal that the retreat thesis has legs.
4. **Founder-couple second-cohort guard** — prevents the "concierge for one tribe" trap that early-stage premium services routinely fall into.
5. **Permanent kill of managed-token resale** — both agents converged that "private space" + "we re-sell your inference usage with markup" is incoherent positioning, not a secondary revenue line.

---

## Weaknesses

- **Geographic TAM never got attacked at the metro level.** Reality-check math in Round 1 showed plausible national addressable couples in the low thousands; neither side translated this into a specific launch-metro number.
- **Symmetry problem.** Reviewer was consistently sharper and shorter; architect did most of the spec-rewriting work. A more balanced debate might have surfaced architect-side counter-attacks on the "drop romance entirely" proposal that never got fully tested.
- **No discussion of pricing elasticity within the SKUs.** The $300 / $500 / $2K bands are stated but never stress-tested against comparable consumer-experience pricing data.
- **The agentic-AI workshop content** got demoted to "optional and visually subordinate" without anyone designing what it actually looks like in the new portable format.
- **No timeline pressure-test.** "90 days" is the kill clock, but seasonal effects on couples-experience purchases (gift season, summer, etc.) weren't considered.

## Implication for the original spec

The v1 spec at `/Users/v/Code_2026/ctc/docs/2026-04-25-couples-that-code-design.md` is **substantially out of date** as of this debate. The lodging marketplace framing, the 5–10 lighthouse properties, the bundled-SKU strategy, and the managed-token revenue line were all retired. Recommended action: write a v2 spec (or a Phase 0 brief) that adopts the locked v1.4 plan from Round 5, and treat the original spec as a brainstorm artifact rather than a current design.

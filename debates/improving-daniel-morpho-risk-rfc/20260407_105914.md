## Converged Recommendations

### 1. Retitle

**From:** "A Morpho-compatible DeFi Collateral Asset Risk Model"
**To:** "Morpho Collateral Risk Transparency Layer"

"Transparency" matches the proof of work. "Risk model" implies calibrated outputs that don't exist yet.

### 2. Three-layer document structure

- **Main RFC** (3-4 pages): problem, proof of work, scoped deliverables, budget, team — for governance delegates
- **Technical appendix**: six-factor model, Merton layer, GARCH/HMM design — for technical reviewers
- **Evidence appendix**: live outputs, incident case studies, claim register — for skeptics and auditors

### 3. Lead with what works today

The RFC currently buries the dependency engine on page 7. It should be on page 1. The wsrUSD analysis (85.8% BTC concentration, 4-5 layers of recursion, circular reflexivity in smokeUSDT) is more compelling than any model description.

**Body order:**
1. Problem (incidents + Prosperi citation)
2. What exists today (live dependency decomposition)
3. What current tools cannot do (Credora differentiation — 3 sentences, not 500 words)
4. What this grant delivers
5. Team
6. Budget and milestones
7. Sustainability

### 4. Shadow mode for quantitative outputs

Publish six-factor scores and PD estimates for 60-90 days without asking anyone to rely on them. Builds track record, enables community stress-testing, signals discipline over promotion.

### 5. Budget: one grant, one vote, one scope

- **Option A:** $150K-$180K / 9 months — transparency as core, shadow-mode modeling as bonus track
- **Option B:** $225K-$250K / 12 months — only if first 6 months fund public infrastructure and second 6 months fund experimental modeling contingent on delivered artifacts

Do not ask for $250K while sounding like a small informal collective.

**Milestone shape (9-month variant):**

| Period | Deliverable | Tranche |
|---|---|---|
| Months 1-3 | Public vault decomposition API, top 20-30 Morpho vaults, standardized dossiers | ~$60K |
| Months 4-6 | Oracle/liquidity/governance alerting, dashboard v1, incident replay tools | ~$60K |
| Months 7-9 | Experimental factor scoring in shadow mode, backtest notebook, open-source release | ~$60K |

Gate tranches on shipped public artifacts, not architecture prose.

### 6. State FTE capacity explicitly

~1.5-2 FTE across four part-time contributors. Say it directly. Delegates are more forgiving of limited scope than fuzzy capacity.

### 7. Named individuals, not "Sigma Labs"

- **Vincent**: DeFi Integration Graph (DIG), oracle price series, leverager contracts
- **Amaury**: yield tree decomposition, archive-node pipeline, rebalancing agent
- **Daniel**: Morpho vault curator (Hadjime USDC Prime), RFC author, BD

Artifacts over pedigree.

### 8. Credora/RedStone: complementary, not adversarial

Three sentences of functional differentiation. Do not pick a public fight with your oracle provider's risk product. Do not mention Odyssey.

### 9. Public-good argument on page 1

"Individual curators can justify building private tooling for their own vaults. They cannot justify building a common transparency layer for all depositors across all vaults. That coordination gap is exactly what DAO grants exist to fill."

### 10. Cite Prosperi structurally

Use "Physics of On-Chain Lending" (April 6, 2026) in the Problem section as independent validation of the 20-100x spread mispricing thesis. External authority as problem-sizing tool.

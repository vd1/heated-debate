## Key Conclusions (Converged)

1. **Retitle**: "A Morpho-compatible DeFi Collateral Asset Risk Model" → **"Morpho Collateral Risk Transparency Layer"**. The current title promises a production risk model that doesn't exist yet.

2. **Lead with what's built, not what's designed**. The dependency graph engine and yield tree decomposition are the proof of work — they belong on page 1, not page 7. The wsrUSD and Sentora PYUSD analyses are more compelling than any model description.

3. **Three-layer document**: Main RFC (3-4 pages for delegates), Technical Appendix (model math for reviewers), Evidence Appendix (live outputs + claim register for skeptics).

4. **Shadow mode** for quantitative outputs: publish factor scores and PD estimates for 60-90 days without asking anyone to rely on them. Builds credibility for Phase 2.

5. **Budget**: $180K / 9 months in 3 delivery-gated tranches (~$60K each). Current $250K/12mo is too ambitious for what the team can credibly commit to as part-time contributors.

6. **Name the team, drop "Sigma Labs"** unless it's a registered entity. Artifacts over pedigree.

7. **Credora: complementary, not adversarial**. Shrink from 500 words to 3 sentences. Don't pick a fight with your oracle provider's risk product.

8. **Public-good argument on page 1**: no single curator is incentivized to build cross-vault transparency for all depositors. That's the DAO grant justification.

9. **Cite Prosperi** (April 6, 2026) as independent validation of 20-100x spread mispricing.

10. **State FTE commitment explicitly**: ~1.5-2 FTE across four part-time contributors.

## Key Disagreements (Resolved)

- Budget sizing: Agent B pushed for $150K-$180K, Agent A for $180K with a Phase 2 option. Converged on single-grant $180K/9mo as the cleanest structure.
- "Observability" vs "Transparency": both agreed "transparency" better captures revealing hidden structure vs monitoring dashboards.

## Best Ideas

1. **Retroactive incident case study** — the single highest-leverage pre-submission artifact. Show with dated onchain state that existing tooling would have flagged USD0++, xUSD, or Resolv/USR before losses.
2. **Claim register** — tag every RFC assertion as `built`, `prototype`, `research design`, or `future work`. The current ratio is ~2 built / 4 designed / 6 future but the RFC reads inverted.
3. **Shadow mode** — 60-90 day non-binding publication of model outputs, enabling community stress-testing.
4. **Six overclaims to soften** — "works on any EVM asset" → "targets top 30 Morpho assets"; "real-time" → specify actual update frequencies; PD estimates → "Phase 2 research objective."
5. **The delegate test**: "What can you show me working this week?" must be answerable from page 1.

## Weaknesses of the Debate

- No engagement with actual Morpho governance precedent (past grant sizes, approval rates, delegate preferences)
- Assumed the team would accept a budget cut without modeling the financial impact on contributors
- Didn't address whether the math reference v3.0 document needs revision alongside the RFC

## Mechanics Report

**Process Score: 5/10**

**Dial Effectiveness:** The creativity dial produces a visible structural arc — Round 1 is expansive with bold claims, tables, and radical reframings (appropriate for Wild 5/5), while later rounds progressively codify and freeze vocabularies (appropriate for Precise 1/5). However, the cooling happens too fast: by Round 3 the debate is substantively finished, making the dial's effect on Rounds 4-5 moot since there is nothing left to compress. The dial shaped the first three rounds well but had no meaningful work to do in the final two.

**Convergence Quality:** Convergence is the central process failure. Agent B's Round 1 'counterargument' is already a 'yes, and...' refinement rather than genuine opposition. By Round 2, both agents agree on the winner, the runner-up, and the core object model. Rounds 3-5 are collaborative spec-writing, not debate. The final two rounds produce near-identical outputs — Agent B's Round 5 is essentially a copy of Agent A's Round 5 with trivial rewording. Genuine intellectual tension exists for roughly 1.5 rounds out of 5.

**Contract Adherence:** Both agents consistently produce verdicts, runner-ups, and reasoning structures. They engage with each other's specific points (the subsidy-as-leg-type correction, the realization field addition, the edge-strength proposal). The 7-case decomposition table and practical rulebook formats are maintained. However, neither agent meaningfully challenges the other's premises after Round 1, which undermines the 'debate' contract. The format is followed; the adversarial spirit is not.

**Agent Dynamics:** Agent A consistently leads with proposals; Agent B consistently plays the 'tightening reviewer' role — accepting the core thesis and adding refinements (realization field, edge strength, service/transfer superclass split). This produces a monotonic refinement arc rather than a dialectic. Agent B never seriously contests Agent A's framing, never proposes a fundamentally different winner, and never forces Agent A to defend a position. The dynamic is 'architect + copy-editor,' not 'advocate vs. advocate.' For a 'heated debate' engine, this is too cooperative by at least 2 rounds.

**Circular Arguments:**
- 'Winner remains Framework E as Leg Ledger Graph / Runner-up remains D' is stated identically in Rounds 3, 4, and 5 by both agents without new justification — pure repetition.
- The frozen schema (leg/edge/wrapper) is reproduced verbatim across Rounds 4A, 4B, 5A, and 5B — four consecutive repetitions of the same code block with no material changes.
- The operational rules list ('split a leg whenever...', 'treat leg_type as accounting form only...', 'use unknown or mixed...') is copy-pasted across the last three rounds by both agents.
- 'Do not use wrapper fields to repair a bad leg split' appears in Rounds 3A, 4A, 4B, 5A, and 5B as if it were a new insight each time.

**Off-Topic Drifts:**
- No significant off-topic drifts detected. Both agents remain tightly focused on the decomposition question throughout. The debate is disciplined in scope, even if it converges too quickly.

**Recommendations:**
- Introduce a mandatory 'steelman the opposition' requirement in early rounds — force each agent to seriously argue for a different winner before converging, especially at high creativity settings.
- Add a convergence detector that ends the debate early when agents produce >80% overlapping content, rather than forcing redundant rounds that waste tokens and dilute signal.
- Give Agent B a stronger adversarial mandate — e.g., 'you must identify at least one fundamental flaw in the other agent's proposal that would change the verdict, or explicitly justify why none exists.'
- Reduce the round count for topics where the solution space is narrow, or add a 'divergence injection' mechanic in middle rounds (e.g., introduce a new constraint or edge case that forces re-evaluation).
- Penalize verbatim repetition of schemas, rules, or verdict language from prior rounds — require each round to add net-new analytical content or explicitly signal that the debate has concluded.

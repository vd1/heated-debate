## Substance Report — Iteration 2

### Settled Conclusions

- The 4-source folk taxonomy (farming/staking/arb-fund/RWA) **mixes ontological layers** and is not adequate for portfolio risk analysis
- The unit of classification must be **one cash-flow leg from the holder's perspective**, not one position
- **Payer must be split** into "immediate payer" and "ultimate funding source" — this mechanically generates dependency chains rather than treating them as afterthoughts
- **Risk must be split** into primary short-risk, secondary short-risks, and universal overlays
- Rate dynamics / yield regime is an **overlay**, not a co-equal base layer
- **Gross vs net separation**: classify gross cash-flow legs separately; hedges, slippage, rebalancing, gas, and mark-to-market losses go in "net-return drags"
- **Stopping rule for dependency chains**: trace ultimate funding source until hitting a terminal category (external cash flow / protocol treasury / user demand / zero-sum transfer), stop after 2 hops unless a third is needed to expose a subsidy

### Best Ideas

- **B+D+C layered protocol**: Framework B (Payer x Mechanism) as the analytical backbone, Framework D (Risk-first) for portfolio attribution, Framework C (Economic Character) as per-leg sustainability overlay
- **Immediate vs ultimate payer distinction**: "the real enemy is not blended products; it is confusing immediate cash payer with ultimate economic sponsor"
- **Case-forced evaluation**: fill the 7-case decomposition table first, then use disagreements in the table as debate content — prevents meta-discussion loops
- **MEV solved by perspective field**: MEV gets three rows (validator: ordering-rights revenue; searcher: strategy PnL; user: extraction cost)
- **Morpho dependency chain made explicit**: immediate payer = borrower, ultimate funding source = farming protocol treasury, failure mode = emissions end -> borrowing demand drops -> lending yield collapses

### Open Questions

- Does the 7-case decomposition table actually work in practice? No case was fully classified in this debate
- Is the stopping rule (2 hops) always sufficient? Some chains may be longer
- Does the protocol work in under 5 minutes for a new vault? Or does accuracy with moderate effort matter more than speed?
- Should the debate be re-run with the refined topic to actually produce the decomposition table?

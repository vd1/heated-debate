## Substance Report

**Settled Conclusions:**
- Framework E (composite) implemented as a 'Leg Ledger Graph' is the most useful decomposition for portfolio risk analysis, decisively beating all single-framework alternatives
- Framework A (4-source folk taxonomy: Farming/Staking/Arb-fund/RWA) is rejected — it mixes payer, role, strategy, and asset-origin in one incoherent layer
- Framework D (risk-first) is the best single-view stress projection but insufficient as the base data model because it hides who funds the carry
- The correct object is a data model with three tables (leg, edge, wrapper), not a flat taxonomy of 'yield sources'
- leg_type must describe accounting form only (cash flow, mtm transfer, non-cash incentive), never economic origin — putting 'subsidy' in leg_type reintroduces layer-mixing
- subsidy belongs in mechanism_subclass, keeping accounting form orthogonal to economic origin
- Split a leg whenever accounting form, payer, mechanism, or payoff shape differs — this is the core splitting discipline
- ultimate source tracing must stop within 2 hops at one of five terminal tags: external cash flow, protocol treasury, user demand, zero-sum transfer, unknown
- primary short-risk and break condition are both mandatory on every leg — break condition handles regime-switching risks that a single primary label misses
- wrapper fields must never compensate for a bad leg split — if underlying economics are mixed in one row, the wrapper table cannot save the model
- Use unknown/mixed/low-confidence values rather than forcing false precision, especially for borrower-demand provenance and subsidy dependence
- Mechanism vocabulary splits into two superclasses (service vs transfer) for quick sustainability reads, with ~10 frozen subclasses
- The schema and closed vocabularies should be frozen now, with extension only through explicit versioned additions
- Four canonical portfolio views are defined: risk-first (by primary short-risk), funding-source (by ultimate source), mechanism (by superclass+subclass), and dependency (typed edges)
- Fees are not yield — gross cash-flow source and net-return drags must always be reported separately

**Open Questions:**
- How to operationalize edge_strength assessment — what empirical criteria distinguish 'hard' from 'strong' from 'soft' dependency links?
- How to source the underlying data needed to populate the leg ledger when most DeFi dashboards do not expose leg-level decomposition?
- How to prevent Goodhart effects once protocol teams optimize to the taxonomy by repackaging subsidy as 'borrow demand' or 'market-neutral carry'?
- Whether the mechanism_subclass vocabulary is complete enough for novel DeFi primitives, or whether a versioned extension process will be needed soon
- How to handle products where the 5-minute decomposition threshold fails — beyond flagging opacity as risk, what triage protocol applies?
- Whether the realization field (realized cash, accrued claim, unrealized mtm, in-kind incentive) needs further granularity for on-chain accrual mechanics
- How to encode time-varying edge strength — a 'soft' subsidy link can become 'hard' under stress, and the schema does not yet capture regime-conditional edges
- What governance body or process actually manages vocabulary versioning in practice — who decides when to add a new mechanism_subclass?

**Best Ideas:**
- Stop classifying yield products — classify each yield leg separately by who pays, how it pays, what you are short, and what breaks the thesis
- Model every strategy as both revenue legs and loss legs: Uniswap v3 should have a fee leg (cash flow, service/spot market-making) and an inventory PnL leg (mtm transfer, no external payer), making 'fees are not yield' enforceable in the data model
- Break condition field captures regime-switching risk that a static primary short-risk label misses — e.g. sUSDe looks like funding-regime risk normally but becomes exchange/custody gap risk under stress
- Typed directional dependency edges (funded-by-subsidy, shares-payer-base, shares-collateral, shares-exit-liquidity, shares-curator, depends-on-spread-regime) turn contagion analysis from prose into queryable data
- Service vs transfer mechanism superclass gives instant sustainability signal at a glance before drilling into subclasses
- If a vault cannot be decomposed into legs within 5 minutes, treat opacity itself as a first-class risk signal rather than forcing a classification
- Separate wrapper fragility from underlying economics — Resolv/Morpho-style products have clean underlying mechanisms but messy wrapper-level concentration, correlation, and governance risk that must be modeled independently
- Replace the phrase 'source of yield' with 'cash-flow provenance + short-risk vector' to force analytical precision and eliminate category theater

**Dependency Chains:**
- Morpho lending yield <- borrower interest <- borrower demand <- often emissions/subsidy harvesting elsewhere (1-hop subsidy chain that makes mechanically-productive lending economically subsidy-dependent)
- sUSDe funding leg <- persistent perp long/short imbalance <- market structure regime; sUSDe incentive leg <- ENA treasury <- token price <- reflexive demand (two independent fragility chains that must be reported separately)
- Resolv USR <- cross-venue borrow/funding spreads <- allocator/curator positioning <- redemption pressure (curator exits simultaneously impair multiple underlying legs through shared funding base)
- MEV validator revenue <- builder bids <- searcher extraction <- user/trader activity via zero-sum transfer (must be split into validator-access and searcher-extraction legs or user harm gets smuggled into 'network yield')
- Wrapper-level exit liquidity <- underlying leg utilization <- shared payer base (apparently diversified legs in a wrapper can share one fragile funding source, making wrapper diversification fake)

**Concrete Proposals:**
- Standardize a Leg Ledger Graph with three canonical objects: leg (leg_type | realization | direction | immediate payer -> ultimate source | mechanism_superclass | mechanism_subclass | primary short-risk | secondary risks | gross source | main drags | break condition), edge (from_leg -> to_leg | edge_type | edge_strength), wrapper (routing control | redemption priority | concentration | hidden correlation | governance control | opacity)
- Freeze these closed vocabularies: leg_type (cash flow, mtm transfer, non-cash incentive), realization (realized cash, accrued claim, unrealized mtm, in-kind incentive), direction (inflow, outflow), mechanism_superclass (service, transfer), mechanism_subclass (validation, spot market-making, floating balance-sheet rental, term balance-sheet rental, insurance risk warehousing, volatility risk warehousing, ordering-rights extraction, liquidation capture, subsidy, offchain pass-through), ultimate source (external cash flow, protocol treasury, user demand, zero-sum transfer, unknown), primary short-risk (inventory, credit, duration, liquidity, volatility/convexity, governance/dilution, operational/counterparty, unknown), edge_strength (hard, strong, soft)
- Implement four portfolio aggregation views: Risk-first (aggregate by primary short-risk for stress testing), Funding-source (aggregate by ultimate source for sustainability analysis), Mechanism (aggregate by superclass+subclass for strategy exposure), Dependency (inspect typed edges for contagion and shared funding bases)
- Report every vault as a waterfall: gross legs -> ultimate funding tags -> primary short-risks -> main drags -> dependency flags, with mandatory break conditions on every leg
- Enforce six operational rules: (1) split legs aggressively by form/payer/mechanism/payoff, (2) leg_type is accounting form only, (3) ultimate source stops at 2 hops, (4) primary short-risk mandatory, (5) break condition mandatory, (6) unknown/mixed preferred over false precision

**Prior Constraints (for next iteration):**

The following are established:
- The most useful decomposition of DeFi yield for portfolio risk analysis is NOT a flat taxonomy. It is a data model called the 'Leg Ledger Graph' with three objects: leg table, edge table, and wrapper table.
- The 4-source folk taxonomy (Farming/Staking/Arb-fund/RWA) is rejected as incoherent — it mixes payer, protocol role, strategy class, and asset origin in one layer.
- Framework D (risk-first) is the best single stress projection but insufficient alone because it hides funding provenance and sustainability.
- The canonical leg schema is: leg_type | realization | direction | immediate payer -> ultimate source | mechanism_superclass | mechanism_subclass | primary short-risk | secondary risks | gross source | main drags | break condition.
- The canonical edge schema is: from_leg -> to_leg | edge_type | edge_strength.
- The canonical wrapper schema is: routing control | redemption priority | concentration | hidden correlation | governance control | opacity.
- leg_type must describe accounting form only (cash flow, mtm transfer, non-cash incentive), never economic origin.
- Mechanisms split into two superclasses: service (validation, market-making, lending, insurance, offchain pass-through) and transfer (subsidy, ordering-rights extraction, inventory transfer, liquidation capture).
- ultimate source tracing stops within 2 hops at: external cash flow, protocol treasury, user demand, zero-sum transfer, or unknown.
- primary short-risk and break condition are both mandatory on every leg.
- Wrapper fields never repair a bad leg split.
- Fees are not yield — gross source and net drags must always be separated.
- Products must be split into legs whenever accounting form, payer, mechanism, or payoff shape differs (e.g., Uniswap v3 = fee leg + inventory PnL leg; sUSDe = funding leg + incentive leg; MEV = validator leg + searcher leg).
- Four portfolio views are defined: risk-first, funding-source, mechanism, and dependency.
- Vocabularies are frozen; extension requires explicit versioning.
- unknown/mixed/low-confidence values are preferred over false precision.

## The Core Flaw

**On modular arithmetic:** 1+1≡0 (mod 2) doesn't refute 1+1=2 in natural numbers. You're not showing that 1+1=2 is false; you're showing that different algebraic structures have different properties. **On axioms and conventions:** Yes, math rests on agreed-upon axioms. But "resting on axioms" makes a claim *more* true (logically necessary given premises), not less. Once you accept Peano axioms, 1+1=2 follows with absolute certainty. That's not weakness; that's proof. Empirical facts have less certainty than this.

**On Intuitionism:** Intuitionist mathematics doesn't deny 1+1=2. It demands constructive proof—which is trivially satisfied here. You've picked the wrong framework to undermine the claim.

**On category theory:** Category theory requires explicit specification (which category?), but it doesn't abolish universal truths—it clarifies them. In the category of natural numbers, 1+1=2 everywhere, always.

**On your "philosophical gain":** Recognizing that math is axiomatic is useful. But then you overreach and claim this makes 1+1=2 merely "conventional" or "context-dependent." If it's true within every relevant context by definition, it's not context-dependent—it's *robustly universal*.

## The Real Problem

Your position conflates two claims:
- **(A)** "Mathematical truths depend on axiom choice" ✓ True

You've chosen not to defend **(B)**, which is the only one that matters. And you can't—because once the axioms are set, 1+1=2 is absolutely, not conditionally, true.

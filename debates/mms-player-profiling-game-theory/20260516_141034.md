# MMS Player Profiling And Game-Theoretic Morpho Blue Analysis

We want to extend `mms` from a market snapshot/plotting tool into a behavioral analysis system for Morpho Blue. The goal is to profile recurring borrowers ("players"), infer their risk appetite and strategy classes, identify the basket of markets each wallet uses, and estimate how they react over time to changes in carry, borrow rates, utilization, collateral APY, price moves, and liquidation distance.

Debate the best architecture and research program for this extension. Agent A should argue for a concrete, ambitious build plan. Agent B should challenge feasibility, data quality, game-theoretic identification, and failure modes. Both agents should converge on a staged implementation that can start with the current `mms` codebase but eventually support wallet-level strategy labels, market migration maps, reaction functions, crowding/externality metrics, and "who exits first" simulations.

The debate should explicitly address:

1. Data model: what historical panel should be built (`wallet x market x time`), at what cadence, and with which raw/derived fields.
2. Player profiling: which wallet-level features are robust enough to infer risk appetite, strategy, carry sensitivity, and rebalancing style.
3. Game-theoretic mechanics: how to model borrower interactions, rate impact, crowding, exit pressure, and strategic externalities without overclaiming causality.
4. Implementation sequence: what should be added first to the current scripts, what should be deferred, and what outputs/decks/dashboards are worth building.
5. Validation: how to test labels and reaction models against observed future behavior, liquidations, migrations, and rate regime changes.
6. Product shape: whether this becomes a research notebook pipeline, a recurring report generator, a wallet intelligence database, or an interactive dashboard.

The final debate output should include:

- A concrete staged roadmap for `mms` v2.
- A minimal viable schema for snapshots, wallet profiles, market panels, and action events.
- A list of player archetypes and the evidence required to assign each label.
- A careful statement of what game-theoretic claims are defensible vs speculative.
- The first three implementation tasks that should be done in the repo.

**Player Archetypes**

- **Passive Levered Farmer**
  - Evidence: long-lived position, few rebalances, stable market, positive carry, moderate/high M.

- **High-Leverage Maximizer**
  - Evidence: persistent `M_to_Mmax` near 1, thin liquidation buffer, high phi exposure, frequent collateral maintenance.

- **Carry Tourist**
  - Evidence: enters after carry improves, exits after carry compresses, short holding periods, market switching.

- **Rate-Sensitive Deleverager**
  - Evidence: repays or closes after borrow APY/utilization spikes, especially without major price movement.

- **Liquidation-Edge Player**
  - Evidence: repeatedly operates near liquidation threshold, adds collateral only after price stress, survives close calls.

- **Market Migrator**
  - Evidence: closes one market and opens economically similar exposure elsewhere within short window.

- **Whale Externality Driver**
  - Evidence: large borrow share, measurable contribution to utilization/rate, exit/entry materially shifts market state.

- **Noise / One-Off Borrower**
  - Evidence: short history, single market, no repeated behavioral pattern. Should not receive strong labels.

**Defensible Vs Speculative Claims**

**Defensible**

- This wallet repeatedly used these markets.
- This wallet historically maintained this leverage and liquidation-distance range.
- This wallet reduced exposure after these observed market/rate/price changes.
- These wallets are most exposed to a given price or borrow-rate shock.
- A new borrow of size X would mechanically affect utilization/rates under the market‚Äôs IRM.
- A rate increase mechanically lowers high-M borrower carry more than low-M borrower carry.

**Speculative**

- The wallet‚Äôs true intent or risk preference.
- That borrow-rate changes caused an exit.
- That wallets are strategically coordinating.
- That observed migration proves preference for a specific curator, vault, or market design.
- That historical reaction functions will hold in a crisis.
- That a wallet maps cleanly to one human or institution.

### Agent B ¬∑ `gpt-5.5` ¬∑ 46s

The proposal is directionally good, but still over-trusts wallet labels and snapshot diffs. The core risk is building a polished behavioral story on weak identity and sparse timing.

**Main Counterarguments**

1. **Wallet profiling is the weakest unit**
A wallet is not necessarily a player. It may be a vault, safe, bot, relayer, subaccount, liquidation helper, or disposable address. Strong labels like ‚Äúrisk appetite‚Äù should be downstream of an entity-resolution layer, not assumed from address history.

Better primitive: `position trajectory`, then `wallet trajectory`, then maybe `entity cluster`.

2. **Daily snapshots are insufficient for reaction models**
Daily cadence can support descriptive panels, but not serious reaction functions. Rate spikes, repay/borrow loops, liquidation saves, and migrations can happen within minutes. If v2 wants ‚Äúwho exits first,‚Äù it needs event-sourced on-chain logs or at least hourly snapshots around volatility/rate events.

Daily is fine for:
- recurring wallet detection
- market baskets
- leverage distribution
- slow migration maps

Daily is weak for:
- response lag
- causality
- liquidation defense behavior
- exit ordering

3. **Action reconstruction from diffs is dangerous**
Snapshot diffs cannot reliably distinguish:
- repay vs liquidation
- close vs migration vs API disappearance
- add collateral vs collateral price appreciation
- deleveraging vs debt valuation change
- same strategy moving through a new wallet

So `action_events` should separate `observed_events` from `inferred_events`. Confidence scores are not enough unless the schema preserves provenance.

4. **Game theory should be downgraded**
Most game-theoretic claims here are mechanical stress accounting, not strategic equilibrium. That is okay. Call it:
- crowding pressure
- marginal rate externality
- exit pressure
- shock propagation

Avoid pretending to estimate beliefs, coordination, or strategic sophistication unless there are repeated, testable deviations from myopic carry/risk behavior.

5. **APY/carry is too fragile to anchor labels**
Carry tourist and rate-sensitive labels depend on noisy borrow APY, collateral APY, rewards, and oracle-derived collateral yield. The system needs rate provenance and uncertainty bands. A 50 bps collateral APY error at high leverage can dominate the inferred strategy.

Do not build ‚Äúwallet intelligence‚Äù first. Build a **Morpho behavioral flight recorder**:

- event-sourced position histories
- raw market state timeline
- position-level stress metrics

The differentiated product is not ‚Äúthis wallet is a Carry Tourist.‚Äù It is: ‚Äúunder three prior carry compressions, this position type delevered within N blocks, while this cohort did not.‚Äù

Are the following macro vectors real, are any missing, and how can we improve on this 50-year view of the world?

We identify three structural 50-year vectors shaping the world's trajectory. Vector 1 is escalating violence: civil wars, international wars, and resource conflicts increasingly tied to specific stakes — rare earths, oil, gas, wealth, land, wood — as diplomacy and commercial deals are progressively replaced by brute force. Vector 2 is environmental collapse: greenhouse gas emissions, climate disruption, pollution (particularly plastics), the progressive uninhabitability of highly populated zones, and the resulting intensification of migration flows. Vector 3 is the widening capital-labor divide: returns on capital systematically outpacing returns on work, creating a structural class split between a capital class (wealth above ~$5M, generating passive income of ~$200k/year) and workers, however skilled or well-compensated. These vectors form feedback loops — resource scarcity from environmental degradation fuels conflict, conflict accelerates environmental destruction, and the capital class can insulate itself from both, reducing its incentive to address either.

Overlaid on these are shorter-term 5-year vectors — election cycles (Orban, projected Trump midterm defeats), shifting diplomatic alliances (NATO expansion, Canada building middle-power coalitions), and institutional signals (UN resolutions, SDG progress) — which may create the illusion of course correction while the structural trends continue underneath, or conversely accelerate them.

Debate whether these vectors are real and well-characterised, identify any missing macro vectors, and propose improvements to this 50-year framework.

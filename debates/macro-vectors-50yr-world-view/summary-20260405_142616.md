## Key Conclusions (converged)

- The original three vectors (violence, environmental collapse, capital-labor divide) are **real phenomena but misframed** — they are system outcomes, not structural drivers.
- The correct architecture is **layered**: structural drivers → system outcomes → wild cards.
- **Rank order spine:** Demographics and state capacity are the slow constraints; energy transition and AI are the fast multipliers.
- The future is shaped less by smooth trend arrows than by **thresholds, substitutions, and coordination failures**.
- The framework must be **falsifiable** and tracked through measurable indicators, not narrative momentum.

## Key Disagreements (resolved)

- Whether "institutional dissolution" works as a single master dynamic (resolved: no — split into state capacity + legitimacy/epistemics as subcomponents under one driver).
- Whether "near-zero adaptation" was defensible (resolved: softened to "weak, fragmented, and insufficient").
- Petrostates language (resolved: "severe fiscal pressure" not "strand").

## Best Ideas

1. **Layered model replaces vector triad:** Drivers (demographics, energy, AI, state capacity) → Outcomes (coercion, biosphere stress, power concentration, displacement) → Wild cards (AGI, biotech, geoengineering).
2. **Biosphere stress as a race condition**, not monotonic decline — damage accumulation vs. mitigation curves, with threshold crossings as the critical events.
3. **Migration elevated to standalone outcome** — the primary transmission channel through which structural pressure becomes domestic political crisis.
4. **Power concentration reframed** beyond capital-vs-labor to include informational, coercive, and adaptive power — with AI potentially collapsing the capital/labor distinction entirely.
5. **Communications layer derived from analytical model** — briefable version for policymakers without confusing the brief with the analysis.

# Concepts vs concrete manipulations in the SSI cours

**Context.** A 30-minute workshop for executives of French PME/ETI (50–500 employees) who want to pilot AI-agent adoption in their teams. The course teaches the "SSI" framework (segmentation, external memory, repositories, packaging — proposer/verifier pattern, acceptance criteria). The tool used in the demo is Cowork (Claude's desktop app), but the framework is meant to be tool-agnostic (also applies to Claude Code, Codex CLI). Two canonical examples carry the framework: (1) a 15–25 page strategic memo from heterogeneous sources; (2) a reusable workflow that automates a recurring task. Audience are not developers.

**The question.** Should the cours teach a set of explicit concepts (the SSI framework, named and explained), or should it just encourage and walk through concrete manipulations (do this, then this, then this) and let the concepts emerge implicitly?

**The tension.**
- **Concepts as spine:** survives tool changes (the framework outlives Cowork), enables transfer to new use cases, gives execs a vocabulary to pilot adoption in their teams. Risk: abstract, doesn't stick without doing.
- **Concrete manipulations as spine:** immediate value, easier to follow in 30 min, execs leave with something they can repeat. Risk: tied to current Cowork UX, doesn't transfer when tools change, execs can't generalize.

**Current doc stance.** "On n'enseigne pas Cowork; on enseigne SSI en utilisant Cowork" — concepts as spine, manipulations as carrier. But the real lever is *ratio and sequencing within those 30 minutes*: how much explicit framework vs how much "watch me do it, here's the concept landing as it happens"?

**Debate this.** Is concepts-as-spine the right call for this audience and format, or is it a teacher's bias that will lose the room? If concepts-first is right, what's the minimum viable explicitation (a slide? a sentence? an aside?) that earns its place in a 30-min slot? If manipulations-first is right, how do you avoid leaving execs without transfer vocabulary?

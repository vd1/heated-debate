# Paragraph under review

Establishing a neutral institution to support quantum technology hardware procurement is essential to counterbalance the lobbying pressures inherent in any high-stakes public acquisition. However, neutrality can be enacted in two fundamentally different ways. The first — and weaker — approach is to constitute the evaluating body as a closed silo, restricted to vendors' public documentation and formal application files, walled off from any direct technical engagement. While this appears rigorous on paper, it produces an entity that is structurally blind: cut off from active scientific collaboration, it will steadily lose relevance, lack the depth to distinguish genuine capability from polished marketing, and ultimately deliver mediocre assessments. The second approach is *active neutrality*, in which the institution maintains high-coverage engagement across all vendors and substrates — co-developing verification techniques, contributing to benchmark evaluation, and participating in the open scientific process surrounding quantum engineering. Here, neutrality is not achieved by ignorance but by even-handed, hands-on, open-box engagement with the low-level realities of the technology. This distinction matters especially for quantum hardware, which differs from other frontier procurements — say, defence platforms or semiconductor fabs — in that the field is pre-consolidation, with multiple competing physical substrates (superconducting, trapped-ion, photonic, neutral-atom, topological) whose relative merits remain genuinely unsettled at the scientific level. No amount of paperwork review can adjudicate between them; only deep technical fluency can. An institution grounded in peer-reviewed, open-source evaluation and direct engineering engagement will be equipped to apply procurement criteria — including strategic, industrial, and operational considerations that may diverge from purely scientific rankings — with the authority and discernment that the decision demands. Active neutrality is a far stronger foundation for sound procurement than neutrality born of not knowing much.

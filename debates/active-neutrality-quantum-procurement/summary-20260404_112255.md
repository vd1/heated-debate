### Key conclusions (converged)
- The original paragraph correctly diagnoses the need for deep technical fluency in quantum hardware procurement
- Its prescription (a single "actively neutral" institution) is fatally underspecified: it optimizes for competence while hand-waving independence and auditability
- The false binary (closed silo vs active engagement) conceals the harder middle ground of structured independence with controlled access
- **Replacement architecture**: two layers + firewall — (1) public technical evidence consortium that publishes but doesn't recommend, (2) procurement decision function that decides but doesn't co-develop, (3) enforceable transparency/recusal/challenge rules between them
- Capture risk is reduced, never eliminated — framing should be honest about this
- Quantum hardware's pre-consolidation state may warrant portfolio/staged procurement, not single-winner selection
- The institution needs a sunset clause tied to technology maturity

### Best ideas
1. **Design triangle** (competence / independence / auditability) as the analytical framework — the original paragraph collapses all three to one vertex
2. **Function separation** as the core mechanism: people who know the most don't decide; people who decide can't suppress evidence
3. **Adversarial challenge procedures** with evidentiary thresholds — not open-ended, but structured replication rights
4. **"Procedural drag as rational hedge"** — reframes bureaucracy cost as insurance against lock-in
5. **Lifecycle/sunset clause** — pre-consolidation is a phase, not a permanent state; the institution shouldn't outlive its justification

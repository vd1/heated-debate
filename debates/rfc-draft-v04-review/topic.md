Review draft v0.4 (self-labelled "v0.5") of the RFC "A Morpho-compatible DeFi Collateral Asset Risk Framework — Phase 1" for submission to Morpho governance. This draft is a major rewrite of v0.3 that attempts to address the v0.3 debate review (included as Document 2 of the context snapshot). The key changes from v0.3 are: (a) reframed from a multi-layer "risk infrastructure stack" to a pure "disclosure / data intelligence layer" — no aggregate scores, no grades, no safe/unsafe verdicts, only categorical badges (e.g. `circular dependency detected`, `single-EOA minting role`, `hardcoded oracle`); (b) the six-factor quantitative risk model (Z₁–Z₆), intrinsic ratings, effective volatility, and PDT are removed from the main body and deferred to a potential follow-on grant under "What Comes Next"; (c) the phased/tranched Phase 1 + Phase 2 structure is collapsed into a single 6-month Phase 1; (d) the subscription-based sustainability model, the 12+12 maintenance commitment, and the insurance-as-downstream-market thesis are all removed, replaced with a simple "MIT-licensed public good, community fork if we fail" exit clause; (e) the budget drops from roughly $200K/12-months to $120K/6-months (the text says Total Grant Request $110K but the table totals $120K — flag this); (f) the USD0++ case study is dropped; only xUSD and Resolv/USR remain; (g) a new "four illusions" framing (collateral independence, TVL, diversification, non-leverage) opens the Abstract; (h) DIG is renamed from "Dependency Graph Engine" to "DeFi Integration Graph"; (i) the Credora positioning is retained and lightly tightened.

Evaluate: (1) whether the v0.3 debate review findings are meaningfully addressed or simply ducked by narrowing scope; (2) whether the "disclosure layer, no scores" repositioning is coherent and defensible, or whether it hollows out the commercial thesis and makes the product indistinguishable from a data-vendor feed; (3) whether the "four illusions" framing is rhetorically effective and factually defensible, or whether it overclaims (e.g. is the "non-leverage illusion" really an illusion for passive vault depositors?); (4) budget and scope credibility at $110K/$120K for six months with no subscription sustainability story; (5) whether dropping the sustainability/exit-clause machinery is a strength (honest) or a weakness (no path to month 7+); (6) whether moving the quantitative model to a speculative "What Comes Next" paragraph undermines the team's Layer-2 credibility, or appropriately right-sizes Phase 1 claims; (7) whether two retroactive case studies (xUSD, Resolv/USR) without USD0++ is sufficient evidentiary backing; (8) the internal consistency of the budget table ($110K headline vs $120K sum), the Month targets, and the scope statement ("top 30 Morpho vaults + ≥5% recursive dependencies"); (9) team presentation — is it now too thin after the restructure?; (10) whether the Manifest template is a credible deliverable or a filler item; (11) the Credora positioning paragraph — still diplomatic and accurate?; (12) what a Morpho governance voter would actually object to in this version.

# Summary: PF3 embeddings vs agentic IQ

Debate of 2026-06-09 20:14. Claude Opus 4.7 (architect, "for") vs Codex gpt-5.5
(reviewer, "against"), 5 rounds, Codex moderator (level 2) and Codex scoring
panel.

## Quality score: 8/10

A strict 8. This was not a shallow back-and-forth: there was real disagreement,
real concession (Agent A retracted "Motion 1 by outcome" as overreach in round
4), and the exchange produced a concrete, implementable architecture rather than
a vibe. The agents corrected each other instead of restating. It falls short of

sharp original question by reframing it, the numeric gates are asserted rather
than derived from the real corpus scale, and the ontology and label-economics
cost was named but never weighed.

## Key conclusions (what they converged on)

A clean four-way taxonomy, with the original motion defeated and a new category
adopted:

- **Motion 1 (raw embedding similarity decides): false.** Cosine, even
  calibrated and family-stratified, cannot emit a shared artefact, role
  compatibility, or a disqualifier. The 102 collisions from the primitive
  experiment are precisely high-similarity, not-applicable pairs; "discusses" and
  "targets" are neighbours in vector space and opposite in verdict.
- **Motion 2 (embeddings prefilter, agent drafts survivors): true** for families
  with thin label support.
- **Motion 3 (embeddings discover aliases and vocabulary): true** as an additive
  role, never a verdict path.
- **Motion 4 (compiled-evidence replacement): the recommended amended path.** The
  quadratic escape is real, but it does not come from embeddings. It comes from
  one-time per-document structured extraction (cost on the order of papers plus
  edges, not papers times edges), compiled into span-grounded certificates and
  typed compatibility rules. Embeddings are demoted to alias discovery and
  shortlisting. Per-candidate LLM drafting retires family by family, behind a
  certificate contract and numeric rollout gates, never globally.

The binding rule both sides accepted: "no certificate, no replacement." Every
production verdict must emit paper_span, edge_span, shared_artefact_ontology_id,
paper_role, edge_role, required_slots, compatibility_rule, disqualifier_rule,
verdict, and calibrated_confidence, or it is rejected at the pipeline boundary.

## Key disagreements (what stayed unresolved)

- **Where the deciding work actually lives.** Codex's deepest point survived the
  whole debate: if the extractor must assign role (targets vs discusses vs
  benchmarks-against) and compatibility, then the hard semantic adjudication did
  not disappear, it moved upstream into extraction, and embeddings are just
  indexing over an agent-produced representation. They agreed to name this a
  fourth category rather than settle whether it is "really" replacement.
- **Whether the long-tail families will ever clear the gates.** Crypto, QEC,
  networking, and assurance are exactly the thinly-labelled families. Left
  explicitly open as empirical; the system may run Motion 4 on primitives and
  Motion 2 on the rest indefinitely.
- **Panel nuance (Devil's Advocate):** the gates were declared sufficient without
  testing label economics, ontology maintenance cost, or whether certificate
  validity actually predicts downstream trust.

## Best ideas (most actionable or novel)

1. **Embed claim-shapes, not papers.** Extract `(artefact, role in {targets,
   discusses, benchmarks-against, cites, dequantises, simulates-classically},
   scope, modality)` once per document, and encode role mismatch as a HARD MASK,
   not a soft cosine. The four canonical traps (review paper, citation-only token,
   classical simulator consuming circuits, "for example" aside) die at the mask
   layer. This single move relocates the anti-hype discipline into the
   representation, and is the strongest idea in the debate.
2. **Mine the existing drafted verdicts as free supervision.** The roughly 540
   already-drafted pairs (432 from PF1 plus 108 from the PF2 body-tier
   experiment) each carry a named artefact, a role, and an explicit disqualifier,
   written once by the agent and currently discarded after use. They are the
   ready training and calibration set, and the 102 dissolved pairs are the most
   valuable structurally-negative signal in the project.
3. **`insufficient-detail` as a first-class verdict, scored separately.** With a
   two-sided pin (coverage at least 70%, insufficient-detail at most 30%) so a
   family cannot earn "replacement" by abstaining on every hard case. As a bonus
   it exposes thin vendor edges as an enrichment work-item instead of silently
   absorbing them into plausible-sounding verdicts.
4. **Family-by-family rollout with coded demotion triggers.** Numeric joint gates
   (Wilson-lower precision, recall, certificate validity, coverage, audit
   agreement, ablation flip rate, confounder precision, minimum label support)
   plus demotion triggers wired to a tested freeze-and-reinstate policy, so the
   audit is not monitoring theatre.
5. **Paired ablations and a confounder corpus as rollout blockers.** Mutate one
   structural feature of a real positive (role, modality, or artefact) and
   require the verdict to flip; and hold out a corpus of natural confounders
   (partial-overlap, nested-method, survey-plus-original, hardware-caveat,
   regime-shift) that the system must clear independently of headline precision.

## Weaknesses (where it was shallow or missed)

- **The crux was softened by reframing.** The sharp question was "can similarity
  replace reasoning?" The resolved answer is "no, but compile the reasoning into
  reusable structures." That is true and useful, but it is a different question.
  Both sides named the move (to their credit), yet the debate ended by redefining
  PF3 rather than validating the embedding bet the user actually posed.
- **The numbers are asserted, not grounded.** 95% precision, 80% recall, n+ at
  least 20 and n- at least 50 per family sound right but are untethered to the
  real corpus. With only 6 genuine positives across 3 papers in the primitive
  experiment, and the families that most need help being the thinnest, no one put
  a number on how many of the 13 families could ever clear the gate. That is the
  practical kicker and it was left blank.
- **Ontology and label economics were named, not costed.** Building and
  maintaining a typed artefact ontology and typed compatibility rules across 13
  families is itself a large, ongoing effort that may rival the drafting cost it
  is meant to replace. The debate asserted feasibility without weighing this.
- **Extraction quality was waved through.** "The new bottleneck" was conceded but
  no one asked whether a cheap extractor can populate (artefact, role, modality,
  noise model) at the assumed accuracy on dense quantum papers. The whole edifice
  rests on that unexamined assumption.

## What this means for agQSL

The motion as posed (embeddings as a PF3 that escapes the quadratic agentic
recourse) does not hold in its strong form: embeddings alone cannot be the
verdict. But there is a credible, low-cost first experiment that needs no ML
stack and no new dependency in the lean venv:

1. Mine the roughly 540 existing drafted verdicts into a structured
   `(artefact, role, modality, disqualifier)` table (stdlib only; the text is
   already written).
2. Write a handful of typed compatibility rules and check whether they reproduce
   the agent verdicts, leave-one-paper-out and leave-one-family-out.
3. If typed rules reproduce the agent on primitives, that is a Motion 4 pilot for
   one family. If they do not, Motion 2 (prefilter) is confirmed cheaply.

Embeddings (and therefore numpy and a vector store) only enter for the Motion 3
alias-discovery layer, which is additive and can wait. The compiled-evidence
idea is testable against the gold set you already hold, today, with the standard
library.

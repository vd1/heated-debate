# Iran War Data Analysis — Context for Debate

Iran has launched **1,840 ballistic missiles**, **4,295 drones**, and **37 cruise missiles** since Feb 28.

## Phase Evolution of Targeting

### Phase 1 (Days 1-5, Feb 28 – Mar 4): Simultaneous saturation
- All 6 GCC countries + Israel hit simultaneously — first time in history
- UAE hit hardest on Day 1 (167 missiles + drones confirmed)
- Day 5 (Mar 4): massive drone surge — "hundreds of drones target Kuwait, Iraq, Saudi Arabia, UAE" (Al Jazeera)

### Phase 2 (Days 6-20, Mar 5 – Mar 19): Declining intensity, Gulf infrastructure focus
- Israel's share of attacks drops
- Drones increasingly target UAE energy infrastructure, Kuwait airports
- Iran claimed strikes aimed "exclusively at US military installations" — despite hitting civilian infrastructure

### Phase 3 (Days 21-37, Mar 20 – Apr 5): Sustained attritional
- Iran explicitly targeting economic pressure points:
  - Kuwait desalination plant + oil refinery hit Apr 3
  - AWS data centers struck in UAE and Bahrain
  - Kuwait airport fuel tanks hit repeatedly
- Israel gets fewer but higher-value strikes (drone factory in Petah Tikva, near IDF Kirya HQ in Tel Aviv)
- Iran's hit rate is *increasing* even as launch rate stays low (RFE/RL)

## UAE-Specific Data
- Wikipedia: 438 BMs + 2,012 drones + 19 CMs at UAE as of Apr 1
- UAE MoD (Gulf News, Apr 3): intercepted 18 BMs + 4 cruise missiles + 47 drones in a single day
- UAE ambassador op-ed (WSJ, Mar 25): "A simple cease-fire isn't enough. We need a conclusive outcome that addresses Iran's full range of threats."
- Emirati military reportedly struck a desalination plant in Iran on Mar 7 — UAE officially denied
- UAE is the dominant drone target (46.8% of all drones), receiving 4.6 drones per missile — suggesting Iran uses cheap persistent drone swarms for sustained economic disruption

## GCC Political Context
- GCC Secretary General stated member states must participate in negotiations
- Saudi Arabia reportedly told Iran that future attacks may elicit "direct Saudi strikes" on Iran (Mar 6)
- Qatar conducted strikes on Iranian targets on Mar 2 (per JINSA) — later denied by both Emirati and Qatari governments
- Oman barely targeted — consistent with neutral posture
- U.S. Arab partners reportedly becoming "more willing to act against Iran" (JINSA Mar 8)

## Diplomatic Context
- Iran's 5 demands include recognition of sovereignty over Strait of Hormuz
- Iran running "de facto toll booth" on Strait of Hormuz since ~Mar 13, charging up to $2M per vessel
- Ceasefire talks ongoing but stalled — Iran calls US proposal "maximalist, unreasonable"
- Trump claims Iran requested ceasefire; Iran denies
- War expected to last "four to six weeks" per White House (as of Mar 25)

## Key Question for Debate
The UAE absorbs nearly half of all Iranian projectiles despite not being the primary belligerent (US and Israel are). The drone-heavy targeting mix (4.6:1 drone-to-missile ratio vs Israel's 0.8:1) suggests a deliberate strategy of sustained economic pressure rather than military degradation.

**Does this pattern indicate:**
1. A deliberate strategy to provoke UAE into a unilateral military response that would fracture GCC solidarity?
2. Simple geographic/military logic (UAE hosts major US bases, is closest Gulf state, has critical infrastructure)?
3. Economic coercion aimed at forcing UAE to pressure Washington for a ceasefire?
4. Some combination of the above?

Consider: If Iran wanted GCC unity, it would spread attacks more evenly. If it wanted to destroy UAE militarily, it would use more missiles. The drone-heavy, infrastructure-focused campaign against UAE specifically looks designed to create maximum political pressure with minimum escalatory risk.
